US military says strikes on 3 boats in the eastern Pacific Ocean kill 8 people
The U.S. military killed eight alleged narco-terrorists in three strikes on drug-smuggling boats as part of a campaign that has killed at least 95 since September, officials said.

The U.S. military attacked three boats accused of smuggling drugs in the eastern Pacific Ocean, killing a total of eight people.
The strikes resulted in three deaths in the first vessel, two in the second, and three in the third boat, according to a military statement.
At least 95 people have now been killed in operations against suspected drug boats as part of Operation Southern Spear.
The Trump administration stated that the U.S. is in conflict with drug cartels amid efforts to curb illegal drug trafficking.
